### Energy — headline risk became physical scarcity

**[Physically confirmed]** Brent settled at $100.69, up 7.0%, and WTI at $92.19, up 6.2%. Houthi forces attacked two Saudi tankers in the Red Sea; Saudi state news confirmed one vessel ablaze. Iran said Hormuz was “completely closed” absent coordination. Gulf loading activity fell to about 2.5mbpd over the latest seven days from roughly 6mbpd over the prior 30 days, although two Chinese VLCCs still exited Bab el-Mandeb. [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/oil-prices-rise-six-week-high-us-iran-tensions-escalate-2026-07-23/)

**[Forecast / policy response]** Reuters sources expect the OPEC+ core to consider a 188kbpd September increase. That is a prospective supply response, not current physical relief, and disrupted routes limit how much nominal production can reach buyers.

**Cross-asset transmission:** oil above $100 → inflation compensation and hike odds higher → nominal/real yields higher → USD stronger → gold and duration equities weaker.
