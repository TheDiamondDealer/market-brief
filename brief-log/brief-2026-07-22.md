## LISTEN

1. **Gold challenged the regime’s cleanest sign-flip.** Gold jumped 1.6% to $4,068.29 while Brent, DXY and both nominal and real yields also rose. **So what:** this is the first session of a haven-override warning; repeat it and clear $4,150 before calling the relationship broken.
2. **The physical oil shock widened to a second chokepoint.** Brent settled $91.01 as two Saudi crude tankers reversed in the Red Sea following Houthi threats; CPC intake was also halted after Black Sea tanker attacks. **So what:** the shock is no longer only Hormuz, making the >$85 acute condition harder to dismiss as rhetoric.
3. **AI equities ignored the discount-rate script.** Nasdaq rose 1.34%, semiconductors more than 5%, S&P 0.85% and Dow 0.76% even as the 10-year reached 4.63%. **So what:** earnings expectations and positioning are temporarily stronger than the duration drag; Alphabet and Tesla now have to validate that resilience.
4. **Lithium delivered a clean bearish trigger.** The Guangzhou contract fell below CNY150,000 to a five-month low as the market priced 2027 oversupply and a potential CATL mine restart. **So what:** near-term demand and inventory draws may slow the decline, but the standing supply-response trigger is now confirmed.
5. **The yen entered a more intervention-sensitive zone.** USD/JPY reached 163.14 while September hike probability rose to 68.2%. **So what:** carry remains functional, but the distance to a policy response is shrinking; the dangerous market signal is still a rapid reversal below 160 with equity stress.
