# Daily Commodities & Markets Brief — 21 July 2026

**Point-in-time:** 08:30 AEST, Tuesday 21 July 2026. Monday global cash closes unless stated.  
**Regime:** **INTACT AND STRENGTHENED — ACUTE ENERGY SHOCK WITH HAWKISH PRICE CONFIRMATION; NOT YET DISORDERLY.**  
**Price confirmation:** Oil → nominal/real yields → gold is now aligned. Equities and copper are the important divergences.

## LISTEN — the overnight story in five lines

1. Monday's cash session supplied the confirmation missing at yesterday's pre-open: Brent held the acute zone at **$89.22**, the US 10-year closed at **4.60%**, the real 10-year rose to **2.35%**, DXY reached **100.98**, and gold stayed below its trigger at **$4,007.91**.
2. The energy shock remains physical, not just a headline premium: Hormuz traffic is severely constrained, tankers were hit or immobilised, US petrol crossed **$4/gallon**, TTF traded above **€60/MWh**, and the US SPR fell to its lowest since March 1983.
3. But Brent's intraday move above $90 faded after a proposed 10-day ceasefire and amid a record **1.35 billion barrels** of crude on water—acute and two-sided, not a clean march to disorderly stagflation.
4. Equities rejected the full risk-off script: S&P 500 **+0.25%**, Nasdaq **+0.55%** and semiconductors **+1.5%**, even as yields rose; earnings can still absorb the macro shock, although Nasdaq remains below its standing trigger.
5. China's unchanged LPR did not repair broad demand, but copper earned asset-specific physical confirmation through nine-month-high imports, a **$100/t** Yangshan premium and depleted inventories. That is copper strength—not yet a China-wide reflation signal.

## MARKET STATE

The live regime is stronger than it was Monday morning because the cash rates channel joined the physical oil signal. The official US 10-year closed at 4.60% and the real 10-year rose four basis points to 2.35%; DXY firmed to 100.98 and spot gold held at $4,007.91. That completes the standing confirmation rule for gold below $4,050 through rising real yields, while the nominal 10-year has reached its own trigger.

The acute disorderly-stagflation cluster is still incomplete. The 10-year is not above 4.75%, DXY is not above 102, and equity breadth improved rather than deteriorated. The correct verdict is therefore **intact and strengthened**, not broken and not disorderly. No rewrite of `regime-state.md` is warranted.

## DIG IN

### Precious metals

- **Gold:** spot fell 0.2% to **$4,007.91**; December futures settled near **$4,015.90**. The standing downside trigger is now price- and macro-confirmed because the real 10-year rose to 2.35%. The active sign-flip persists: war risk is still acting through oil, inflation and rates rather than through a dominant gold haven bid.
- **Silver:** **$56.55**, +1.2%; **platinum:** **$1,592.86**, +0.1%; **palladium:** **$1,258.83**, +0.9%. Their resilience versus gold shows the move is not a uniform precious-metals liquidation; silver is benefiting from industrial/copper strength.

### Battery and bulk

- **Copper:** LME three-month copper rose 0.8% to **$13,633/t** (about $6.18/lb). China refined-copper imports reached a nine-month high; the Yangshan premium hit **$100/t**, ShFE inventories fell to **79,909t**—more than 80% below mid-March—and 56% of LME stock was cancelled. **PRICE-CONFIRMED / PHYSICALLY CONFIRMED**, but supply/import tightness is doing more work than broad China demand.
- **Iron ore:** around **CNY757.50/t**, -0.5%, back below the >CNY760 relationship-warning level. It remains above the CNY740–750 downside zone but did not join copper's confirmation.
- **Nickel:** around **$16,920/t**, -0.2%; above the standing warning zone but back below the $17,000 invalidation line. Upside repair is fading.
- **Lithium / uranium:** no reliable decision-changing daily quote or catalyst was verified; last live references remain roughly CNY152,000/t lithium carbonate and $85.70/lb uranium.
- **Thermal and coking coal:** no fresh benchmark move met the source-quality threshold. Do not infer broad bulk strength from copper.

### Energy

- **Brent:** settled **$89.22**, +1.3%, after touching $91.42; **WTI:** **$83.23**, +0.9%. A proposed 10-day ceasefire clipped the spike, but Houthi threats to Saudi shipping and recent tanker attacks preserve the physical premium.
- Four vessels transited Hormuz on Sunday versus eight Saturday. Record crude on water at **1.35bn barrels** is the main near-term cushion and the reason a $90 intraday breach did not hold.
- **TTF:** briefly exceeded **€60/MWh**, above the €52 trigger; European storage is below 54% versus 64% a year ago. Gulf LNG disruption keeps Europe in the confirmed shock.
- **Henry Hub:** August futures fell 1.8% to **$2.860/mmBtu** on strong production, weak LNG feedgas and ample storage. The US–Europe gas split remains one of the cleanest regime cross-currents.
- US petrol averaged **$4.003/gallon**; the SPR fell **5.1m barrels to 311.4m**, its lowest since March 1983. The consumer and policy transmission is becoming more tangible even while crude-on-water cushions prompt supply.

### Equities

- **S&P 500:** **7,476.09**, +0.25%; **Nasdaq:** **25,661.70**, +0.55%; **Dow:** **51,987.62**, -0.30%; semiconductors +1.5%. Tech breadth improved despite a six-basis-point rise in the 10-year—an explicit divergence from the acute macro signal.
- **ASX 200:** **8,791.30**, -0.06%. Energy led—Woodside +1.44%, Santos +2.08%, Ampol +1.01%—while technology and broad materials were mixed/weak. Resource cushioning is concentrated, not a broad risk-on signal.

### Macro, FX and policy

- **US 10-year nominal:** 4.60% official; **real:** 2.35%. Both rose. The rate leg is confirmed at the trigger but still well below the 4.75% disorderly-regime condition.
- **DXY:** 100.98, +0.15%, inside its zone; **AUD/USD:** hovered around 0.7000 and is now near 0.6994, far from the bearish 0.688 trigger; **USD/JPY:** ~162.47, confirmed above trigger; **AUD/JPY:** roughly 113.7–113.8, consistent with a stretched but functioning carry channel.
- **China:** 1-year and 5-year LPRs were unchanged at 3.00% and 3.50% for a 14th month, as expected. Copper improved on physical data, iron ore slipped and AUD did not break out: the event was **price-diverging by complex**, not a broad stimulus confirmation.
- **Fed:** December hike probability rose to 83% from 73% as more officials kept a hike open. The standing September trigger is unchanged and must be judged only when a reliable September contract probability is available.
- US June Leading Economic Index fell **0.2%** to 99.1. Growth softness is not yet dominant enough to offset the energy/rates channel.

## CROSS-CURRENTS

1. **Oil → policy is now confirmed; oil → equities is not.** Nominal and real yields rose, DXY firmed and gold weakened, yet S&P/Nasdaq breadth repaired. Strong earnings guidance is the near-term arbiter.
2. **China → copper has selectively reconnected, China → iron/AUD has not.** Copper's inventories and premiums validate physical tightness; unchanged LPRs, softer iron ore and a flat AUD reject a broad domestic-demand interpretation.
3. **Global gas bifurcation is widening.** TTF above €60 reflects Gulf LNG risk and low European storage, while Henry Hub at $2.86 reflects ample US supply and lower feedgas demand.
4. **Weak yen is still a pressure valve, not a crisis signal.** USD/JPY above 162 is confirmed by yields and no intervention. A sub-160 reversal alongside weaker equities would mark a carry unwind.
5. **The ASX hedge is narrow.** Energy producers benefit from crude, but imported inflation, weaker technology and mixed materials prevent a broad-index breakout.
