1. **Oil moved from acute to more visibly physical.** Brent closed $94.07 and prompt backwardation widened to $9.26 as five tankers altered course and two strategic straits carried simultaneous threats. **So what:** a surprise 2 million-barrel US crude build did not break the global seaborne signal.
2. **Gold invalidated its bearish trigger without completing the full regime break.** Futures settled $4,151.90 and spot reached $4,165.87 while oil and yields rose for a second day; DXY eased. **So what:** stop treating gold as cleanly rates-negative, but require another joint close with a firm dollar before declaring haven dominance.
3. **The Fed trigger fired through rates.** September hike pricing reached about 76%, official US10Y closed 4.67% and real 10Y 2.39%. **So what:** the inflation/policy spine is strengthening even though the dollar and gold reaction is no longer clean.
