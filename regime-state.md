## What the verdict means

The dominant market transmission is:

**renewed Gulf supply risk → higher oil and product prices → higher inflation and central-bank tightening expectations → higher Treasury yields and stronger USD → weaker gold-duration behaviour and weaker risk assets.**

This is not a generic “risk-off” regime. On 13 July, Brent rose about 4.3% to $79.31 and WTI 4.4% to $74.62, the US 10-year yield traded near 4.58%, DXY held around 101 and Asian equities sold off. Gold fell about 1.2% to $4,072 despite renewed US–Iran attacks ([Reuters global markets](https://www.reuters.com/business/media-telecom/global-markets-global-markets-2026-07-13/); [Reuters gold](https://www.reuters.com/world/india/gold-slides-over-1-oil-surges-strait-hormuz-closure-fears-2026-07-13/)). That joint move confirms that the rates channel currently dominates the haven channel.

“Strengthening” does not mean permanent. Brent is near, but not yet through, the $80 headline zone; the IEA and EIA still assume gradual flow normalisation and later oversupply. A durable peace/flow recovery could remove the regime’s main input quickly ([IEA, 10 July 2026](https://www.iea.org/reports/oil-market-report-july-2026); [EIA, 7 July 2026](https://www.eia.gov/outlooks/steo/)).

## Active sign-flips

These are the relationships the daily and weekly prompts must apply explicitly.

### 1. Geopolitical escalation is currently gold-negative through rates

- **Usual shorthand:** war risk → gold up.
- **Current sign:** escalation → oil/inflation/hike odds/yields/USD up → gold down.
- **Evidence:** 13 July joint move.
- **Invalidation:** gold rises for two consecutive sessions while DXY and US real/nominal yields also rise, or gold closes above its invalidation level despite an oil shock.

### 2. Strong US data can be equity-negative

- **Usual shorthand:** strong growth → equities up.
- **Current sign:** upside activity/inflation surprise → hike odds and yields up → long-duration multiple compression.
- **Most exposed:** Nasdaq/AI leaders, precious metals and rate-sensitive sectors.
- **Invalidation:** positive data lifts cyclicals and breadth while hike odds/yields remain contained.

### 3. Higher oil is not uniformly bullish for resource markets

- **Usual shorthand:** commodity shock → resource complex up.
- **Current sign:** oil producers benefit, but higher fuel costs, rates and weaker downstream margins can weigh on metals, manufacturers and broad equities.
- **Evidence:** energy rose while metals and Asian equities weakened on 13 July.
- **Invalidation:** oil strength broadens into copper, iron ore, AUD and materials on physical-demand evidence rather than inflation alone.

### 4. PBOC easing headlines are not automatically bullish iron ore/AUD

- **Usual shorthand:** China easing → metals/AUD up.
- **Current sign:** policy support requires confirmation in credit, property, infrastructure, steel margins and inventories.
- **Evidence:** LPRs remain low/accommodative, yet iron ore, steel and AUD are not confirming a broad demand upswing.
- **Invalidation:** sequential improvement in credit/property or grid/infrastructure data plus copper and iron ore breaking their confirmation levels together.

### 5. Yen weakness is supportive until it becomes destabilising

- **Usual shorthand:** weak yen → Japanese exporters up.
- **Current sign:** gradual weakness helps translation, but USD/JPY above 162 increases intervention and imported-inflation risk. A reversal can unwind carry globally.
- **Invalidation of the benign view:** a rapid USD/JPY fall through 160 with simultaneous equity/credit stress.

### 6. ASX resilience can coexist with weaker AUD

- **Usual shorthand:** weaker AUD → weaker Australia.
- **Current sign:** weak AUD can boost translated offshore and commodity revenue while energy/materials cushion the index, even as it signals imported inflation and global risk aversion.
- **Invalidation:** banks, materials and energy all weaken together below the ASX decision zone.

## What would shift or break the regime

### Shift toward de-escalation / disinflation

Require a cluster, not one headline:

- Brent closes back below $76 and Gulf tanker/export flows continue to recover.
- September Fed hike pricing falls below 50% and US 10-year nominal yields close below 4.45%.
- DXY falls below 100 while gold and equities regain their confirmation levels.
- TTF and product cracks retreat, showing the consumer-energy impulse is easing.

If at least three occur and persist for two sessions, classify the regime **SHIFTING** toward disinflationary risk-on/neutral.

### Shift toward acute stagflation / disorderly risk-off

- Brent closes above $85 with confirmed physical disruption, not rhetoric alone.
- US 10-year yields close above 4.75% or hike odds reprice sharply higher.
- DXY rises above 102 while equity breadth and credit deteriorate.
- Gold also falls below its lower trigger, confirming forced duration/liquidity pressure.

This would be a stronger, more disorderly version of the current regime.

### Break via haven override or policy capitulation

The regime is **BROKEN** if either path becomes persistent:

1. **Haven override:** gold rises alongside oil, DXY and yields for at least two sessions and closes above its invalidation boundary; or
2. **Policy capitulation:** central banks explicitly de-emphasise the inflation shock, hike pricing collapses and yields/USD fall despite oil remaining elevated.
