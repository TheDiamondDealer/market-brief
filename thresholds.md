## Relationship triggers

### Gold safe-haven override

- **Warning:** gold is positive on a day when Brent, DXY and the US 10-year yield are also positive.
- **Headline trigger:** this joint move persists for two consecutive sessions and gold closes above $4,150.
- **Interpretation:** haven/official demand has overtaken the rates channel; change gold-vs-yields scorecard from `confirmed inverse` to `diverging` and reassess the regime.
- **Invalidation:** gold returns below $4,100 while DXY/yields remain high.

### Yen carry unwind

- **Warning:** USD/JPY drops more than 1.5% intraday from above 162.
- **Headline trigger:** daily close below 160 with simultaneous declines in global equities and widening credit spreads.
- **Interpretation:** yen-funded carry reduction is now the dominant risk channel; do not describe yen strength as benign policy normalisation.
- **Invalidation:** USD/JPY re-enters 160–162 and risk assets stabilise.

### China demand confirmation

- **Warning:** copper closes above $6.30 while iron ore closes above CNY760, or both break lower together.
- **Headline trigger:** same-direction move persists for two sessions and is supported by credit/property/grid/steel or inventory data.
- **Interpretation:** a confirmed upside changes the China relationship from `diverging` to `confirmed`; a confirmed downside establishes broader demand deterioration.
- **Invalidation:** the two commodities diverge or the physical data contradicts price.

### Product-led inflation shock

- **Warning:** Brent remains below $80 but gasoline/diesel cracks and TTF continue higher.
- **Headline trigger:** official inventory/refinery data confirm product shortage while crude time spreads remain soft.
- **Interpretation:** consumer inflation can stay high even without a crude breakout; lead with products, not the Brent headline.
- **Invalidation:** refinery runs and product exports normalise and cracks fall.

## Trigger protocol

1. **Observe:** record the exact benchmark, timestamp and source.
2. **Confirm:** apply the confirmation column; a touch alone stays in “on the radar”.
3. **Explain:** state the regime-adjusted directional logic and affected assets.
4. **Invalidate:** include the exit condition in the same brief.
5. **Reset:** monthly, or immediately after a regime break, benchmark discontinuity or >10% structural repricing.

## Data caveats

- Use the same benchmark through time. Do not mix LME/COMEX copper, spot/futures gold, or thermal/metallurgical coal.
- “Fed hike probability” is vendor/model-dependent market pricing. Cite the provider each day and compare like with like.
- A transparent free live premium-HCC benchmark was not verified during bootstrap; no coking-coal threshold is set until a reliable series is available.
- The daily must pull the US 10-year real yield from the Treasury/FRED close before making a real-yield confirmation claim.
