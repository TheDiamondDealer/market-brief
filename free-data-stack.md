# Free Data Stack

The dashboard should prefer free official data sources and clearly label source time, publication lag and freshness.

## Market charts

- Use free TradingView widgets for embedded chart viewing, top stories and the external economic calendar.
- Treat widgets as sealed visual components. Do not scrape, export or claim access to TradingView market data.
- Use TradingView Lightweight Charts when the dashboard needs custom target lines, event markers or scenario overlays. Lightweight Charts requires a separate data source.

## Scenario engine

The `Scenario Lab` is our own analytical layer. It must use:

1. latest verified research reference price
2. current regime and sign-flips
3. active warning, trigger, confirmation and invalidation levels
4. upcoming official economic events
5. relevant COT, rates, currency and physical-market evidence

Output is conditional scenario analysis, never a guaranteed forecast or trade recommendation.

## Free official APIs and feeds

### Positioning

- CFTC Commitments of Traders public reporting data
- Weekly publication lag must be displayed
- Store historical observations locally to calculate percentiles and changes

### Rates

- US Treasury daily nominal and real yield-curve feeds
- FRED API for historical macro and financial series
- RBA, ECB, BOJ and other central-bank official data where available

### US macro

- BLS public API for CPI, employment and labour series
- BEA API for GDP, income, consumption and national accounts
- Federal Reserve official releases and calendars

### Australia, China, Japan and Europe

- ABS and RBA official releases
- National Bureau of Statistics of China and PBOC releases
- Statistics Bureau of Japan, BOJ and Cabinet Office releases
- Eurostat and ECB official releases

## News

- TradingView Top Stories may be embedded as an external context panel.
- It must not replace the dashboard's interpreted News Feed.
- The interpreted feed is generated by the daily research process and must explain assets to watch, first-order impact, second-order impact, confirmation and invalidation.

## Data integrity

Every module should display:

- source
- observation date
- publication time
- dashboard refresh time
- live, delayed, end-of-day, weekly or monthly classification
- stale-data warning when an update fails

Never silently carry forward a value as current. Preserve the last verified value but label it stale.
